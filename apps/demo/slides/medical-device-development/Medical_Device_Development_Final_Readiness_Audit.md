Target quality dimensions:

- Content completeness
- Interaction
- Layout and visual polish
- Warmth and respect for learners
- Theoretical foundation
- Practical examples
- Fit within 90 minutes

## Delivery Safeguards

Use the deck in three modes:

1. Teach the thesis: a medical device is a system, not only a product.
2. Make learners act: vote on intended use, then design for survival in mixed teams.
3. Give them a portable method: five-family checklist plus one harm / one mitigation.

Keep the pacing rule:

- Slow down on Slides 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 10, 14, 25, 26, 36, 41, 42, and 44.
- Fast-scan framework-name slides: 18, 19, 21, 23, 28, 31, 32.
- If time runs short, preserve the two interactions and final checklist.

## Respect and Warmth Checks

Language intentionally avoids:

- country-to-country comparisons
- country-based competition
- blaming local users
- implying resource-constrained means low quality

Language intentionally emphasizes:

- setting / facility / health system
- system mismatch, not user failure
- resource constraints as design requirements
- local expertise in the room
- safety, maintenance, ownership, and adoption after the pilot
